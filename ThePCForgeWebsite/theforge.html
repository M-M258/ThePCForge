FAQ

What is ThePCForge?

It’s an AI‐powered PC building assistant that tailors computer builds to your unique budget and requirements. Behind the scenes, it collects hardware data from external sources and uses advanced AI to compare and recommend components. This means less manual research for you, since ThePCForge handles the heavy lifting of finding current parts and prices.

How does the PC build recommendation work?

You enter a query (for example, “I want a gaming PC under £1,000”). The system uses GPT to generate refined search questions, ensuring that the subsequent data pulled is both relevant and accurate. It then uses SerpAPI to search external websites for the latest hardware information, including current prices and part availability. Next, GPT filters and interprets this data, producing a concise list of recommended components, such as the CPU, GPU, and Motherboard. Finally, the parts list on the right-hand side is updated so you can easily review each recommended item at a glance.

Can I modify the recommended build?

Yes. The recommendations are a flexible starting point. You can adjust your query to refine specific components or change the overall budget. Each new query triggers the system to re‐fetch data and update the recommendations. This loop continues until you’re satisfied with the parts list that meets your exact preferences.

How often is the hardware information updated?

The system always pulls the latest data from external, trusted sources to ensure that prices and part availability are as current as possible. This frequent update process helps maintain accuracy in the recommendations you receive, giving you reliable and up-to-date information.

How accurate are the recommendations?

The system combines refined search queries generated by GPT with real-time data from external sources, resulting in reliable component suggestions. While every effort is made to ensure accuracy, as it still is an Ai-trained application, errors and mistakes can still be made. We recommend verifying prices and compatibility before finalizing your build to ensure the best outcome.
